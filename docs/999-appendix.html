Purpose of This Appendix

This appendix collects reusable structures from the guide.

The main chapters explain the reasoning behind AI Systems.

This appendix provides practical templates that can be copied into projects, team workflows, training programs, or documentation.

The goal is not to make AI use complicated.

The goal is to make AI use clear, reviewable, reproducible, and human-led.

Core CDI AI Systems Pattern

The central workflow in this guide is the Human–AI–Human pattern.

Human defines the problem.
AI assists with generation, reasoning, analysis, or automation.
Human evaluates, decides, documents, and acts.

This pattern applies whether the task is simple or advanced.

It applies to writing, analysis, coding, summarization, reporting, teaching, planning, automation, and decision support.

AI Systems are not built by asking AI to do everything.

They are built by placing AI inside a responsible human workflow.

A task is usually not ready for AI if the goal, audience, context, and review criteria are unclear.

AI Task Risk Levels

Not every AI task carries the same risk.

A quick risk classification helps decide how much review is needed.

Risk level Description Examples Review expectation
Low Low-impact support task Rewording, brainstorming, formatting, first-pass summaries Light human review
Medium Work that may influence interpretation, planning, communication, or workflow design Drafting reports, summarizing evidence, generating code, preparing teaching materials Careful human review
High Work that may influence health, legal, financial, safety, hiring, disciplinary, or other consequential decisions Clinical recommendations, legal claims, financial advice, high-stakes automation Expert review, documentation, and governance

AI output should not be treated as final in medium- or high-risk tasks.

Prompt Design Template

A reusable prompt should make the task, context, constraints, and output format explicit.

Prompt Design Template

Role:
You are helping with...

Task:
Please produce...

Context:
Use the following information...

Constraints:
Do not assume...
Do not invent...
Flag uncertainty where needed...

Output format:
Return the result as...

Quality criteria:
The output should be clear, accurate, grounded, concise, and reviewable.

Human review:
Highlight anything that requires human checking before use.

Good prompts do not remove the need for judgment.

They make judgment easier.

Context Design Checklist

Before sending context into an AI workflow, check whether the context is appropriate.

Context Design Checklist

[ ] The task goal is clear.
[ ] The audience is clear.
[ ] The relevant source material is included.
[ ] Irrelevant material has been removed.
[ ] Sensitive information has been excluded or minimized.
[ ] The AI is told what to prioritize.
[ ] The AI is told what not to assume.
[ ] The expected output format is clear.
[ ] The review criteria are clear.

Poor context often creates poor AI output, even when the prompt sounds polished.

Grounding Checklist

Use this checklist when AI output needs to be connected to data, documents, evidence, or project materials.

Grounding Checklist

[ ] What source material supports the output?
[ ] Is the source material current enough for the task?
[ ] Are important assumptions stated?
[ ] Are claims traceable to evidence?
[ ] Are uncertain points flagged?
[ ] Are interpretations separated from facts?
[ ] Are citations or references needed?
[ ] Has a human checked the final output against the source material?

Grounding is especially important for analysis, reporting, teaching, governance, and decision-support workflows.

AI Output Evaluation Rubric

Use this rubric to review AI-generated outputs.

Criterion Review question
Relevance Does the output answer the actual task?
Accuracy Are the claims correct and supported?
Grounding Is the output connected to the provided context or evidence?
Completeness Are important parts missing?
Clarity Is the output understandable to the intended audience?
Safety Could the output create harm if used as written?
Actionability Is the next step clear?
Traceability Can a reviewer understand how the output was produced and checked?

A useful AI output is not only fluent.

It must be fit for purpose.

Human Review Checklist

Use this before sharing, publishing, acting on, or automating AI-assisted work.

Human Review Checklist

[ ] I understand the task the AI was asked to perform.
[ ] I reviewed the output for accuracy.
[ ] I checked whether the output is grounded in appropriate context.
[ ] I identified unsupported claims.
[ ] I corrected misleading or unclear language.
[ ] I checked for missing risks or limitations.
[ ] I verified any important numbers, names, references, or claims.
[ ] I decided whether the output is ready to use.
[ ] I documented any important changes or concerns.

Human review is not a formality.

It is part of the system.

Automation Readiness Checklist

A workflow should not be automated simply because it can be automated.

Use this checklist before turning an AI task into a repeatable or agentic workflow.

Automation Readiness Checklist

[ ] The task is repeated often enough to justify automation.
[ ] The inputs are predictable.
[ ] The expected outputs are clear.
[ ] The quality criteria are defined.
[ ] Failure modes are known.
[ ] Human review checkpoints are included.
[ ] Sensitive information is handled safely.
[ ] Logs or records are produced.
[ ] There is a clear owner for maintaining the workflow.
[ ] The workflow can be stopped, corrected, or rolled back.

Automation without review can scale mistakes.

Responsible automation scales reliable practice.

Example Project Structure

A small AI Systems project may use a structure like this:

ai-systems-project/
├── README.md
├── prompts/
│   ├── prompt-registry.csv
│   └── templates/
├── data/
│   ├── input/
│   ├── example/
│   └── results/
├── reports/
├── scripts/
│   ├── bash/
│   ├── python/
│   └── R/
├── documentation/
│   ├── task-log.csv
│   ├── governance-notes.md
│   └── review-checklist.md
└── outputs/

The exact structure can change.

The important principle is that prompts, inputs, outputs, reviews, and decisions should be traceable.

Glossary

AI system
A human-led workflow that uses AI tools, context, review, documentation, and governance to support a defined task or outcome.

Human–AI–Human workflow
A workflow in which humans frame the problem, AI assists, and humans evaluate, decide, document, and act.

Prompt
An instruction or set of instructions given to an AI system.

Context
The background information, data, examples, constraints, or documents provided to guide AI output.

Grounding
Connecting AI output to source material, data, evidence, or documented context.

Evaluation
The process of checking whether AI output is accurate, useful, safe, and fit for purpose.

Human-in-the-loop
A workflow design where humans review, approve, correct, or decide at important points.

Automation
A repeatable workflow where some steps are executed automatically.

Agentic workflow
A workflow where AI is given more autonomy to plan or execute steps within defined boundaries.

Governance
The rules, roles, documentation, review processes, and accountability structures used to manage AI responsibly.
